Lindsey Kwok

CIERA Postdoctoral Fellow

Hi, I'm Lindsey! I'm a CIERA Postdoctoral Fellow at Northwestern University. I guess you could call me a stellar sleuth. I investigate the violent deaths of stars by observing and analyzing the light signatures from their explosions and then use models to reconstruct their possible progenitor systems. Most recently, I've been studying how white dwarfs explode using JWST.

I also love sharing what I learn through teaching and outreach. If you'd like to learn more about me, my research, or my teaching, please have a look around my website or contact me.

Research

Supernovae with JWST

I am leading several JWST programs to obtain novel infrared (IR) spectra of white dwarf supernovae. The IR behavior and evolution of supernovae can distinguish between progenitor systems and explosion mechanisms.

  • More

Photometry & Spectroscopy

I analyze the light curve and spectroscopic evolution of supernovae in the UV, optical, & IR. This observational data holds a wealth of information about the physical properties of a supernova.

  • More

TARDIS Modeling

I model supernova spectra using TARDIS, a radiative transfer code, to reconstruct the ejected material's structure and composition.

  • More

Observing with Telescopes

I observe the night sky with telescopes, including remotely operating Keck on Mauna Kea. I have experience planning, executing, and reducing supernova spectroscopy with JWST, the Southern African Large Telescope (SALT), and Keck LRIS+NIRES.
